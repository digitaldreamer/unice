My Role: User research, UX Design, Demo with HTML
Summary: a responsive website for the Cooper-Hewitt Museum

This project was presented at Cooper Hewitt, National Design Museum. The theme of the collections and exhibitions was to explore approximately 240 years of design aesthetic and creativity. The museum has one of the world’s largest collections of decorative arts.

OBJECTIVE
To enhance the reach and engagement with the BLOG | Cooper-Hewitt museum collection with Art enthusiasts.

Live Site: http://www.cooperhewitt.org/

CONCEPT

To give a digital taste of the museum collections, differ from the traditional search and browse mode, and engage in a process of open-ended exploration and sharing.

GOAL

Increasing the awareness and momentum around the museum.

– To engage and interact with potential museum goers

– To improve the user experience and user interface – focus on the basic human senses and cognitive feelings to give users the opportunity to fully appreciate the artwork and collections from beginning (home – responsive website: online experience) to end (museum: physical experience).

  • Giving full accessibility
  • Humanizing messaging and service
  • Creating informational and intuitive paths
  • Appreciating the audience’s diversity and differences
  • Surprising the users
  • Giving a sense of the physical place
User research

The days of each month are lined up in a column, with the top of the column featuring a roundel with all articles. Visitors can experience a full range of new interactive capabilities, including the family festival for design lovers of all ages!

THE 10 THINGS THAT ARE IMPORTANT

  • Facilitate an open-ended exploration through intuitive paths.
  • Make the collection and events relevant to this specific audience.
  • Playful, live, and emotional experience.
  • Provide full accessibility: User-friendly platform.
  • Using audio as a powerful tool to engage with the user.
  • Push the audience to share their memories.
  • Increase the context (world) around the object.
  • Encourage social interactions (building a community).
  • Producing an appealing and intuitive platform respecting the universal design.
